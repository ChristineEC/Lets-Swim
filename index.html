Why learn to swim?

Swimming is an important survival skill

According to the Centers for Disease Prevention and Control (CDC), drowning is the number one cause of death for children 1 to 4 years old in the United States.

And for people with autism, both children and adults, drowning is a leading cause of death, according to the American Red Cross.

Swimming is good for you

Just a few of the many health benefits:

  • It's good for your heart and lungs.
  • It's a good full-body workout, engaging many muscle groups and improving core strength.
  • It's low-impact, thus easy on the joints and great for people with arthritis or fibromyalgia.

Swimming is fun!

  • It can be enjoyed together with family and friends of all ages and skill levels.
  • At the beach, by a lake, in a pool, or even just a backyard inflatable wading pool for kids.

About Us

We are a mother-and-daughters team of Red Cross certified lifeguards and water safety instructors, with more than three decades of competitive swimming experience between us. What we all enjoy most about being good swimmers is the ability to enjoy a wide variety of water activities, from pool swimming to ocean swimming, and especially bodysurfing.

But you don't have to be an swimmer to enjoy the water! The most important thing is water safety.

I first had the idea to someday offer lessons in my own home many years ago, when a friend asked me to teach her how to swim. Her religious beliefs made learning to swim in public problematic due to the lack of privacy and proper swimming attire available. Today the burkini has been fairly well adapted to water activities. Nevertheless, learning to swim in such attire is much more difficult.

We offer a safe space for people from all walks of life to enjoy the wonderful world of aquatics.

Why learn how to swim?

Swimming is an important survival skill

While my daughters teach swimming as a side-job to their studies, I do it in my free time to make swimming lessons more accessible to people in my community.

There are any number of reasons that swimming lessons offered in our local community can be (or feel) inaccessible to people: water temperature, scheduling conflicts, overstimulating environments, lack of privacy, and so on.

Our lessons are for people of any age or ability. We set up each lesson in consultation with you and adapt the conditions to meet your needs.

Contact us directly or, better yet, use our contact form to tell us a little about your needs.

Accommodations

A few of the accommodations we offer:

  • Quiet hour: for students who need calm and quiet in order to participate
  • Total privacy: for people who want to stay covered up (burkinis welcome) or out of the public eye
  • Bring your own teacher or aide: for students who may benefit (special pricing available)
  • Discounts for siblings
  • Discounts for low-income families
  • Assisted pool entry and exit
  • Warm water temperature and a high pool cover for chilly days
